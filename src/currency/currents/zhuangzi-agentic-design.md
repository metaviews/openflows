### Signal

https://zhuangzi.jessehirsh.com/

Jesse Hirsh’s project **Zhuangzi** presents a *bilingual manifesto of care, agency, and relation* titled "Agentic Design: A Manifesto of Care and Alignment" (行動設計 關懷與協同宣言). It frames AI agent architecture through the lens of Daoist philosophy, proposing "Ink Balanced Motion" as a paradigm for ethical and balanced autonomous systems.

### Context
In the current landscape of AI agent development, the dominant narratives focus on capability scaling, efficiency, and autonomy. This project emerges as a philosophical counter-current, grounding the design of agentic systems in ancient wisdom. It bridges the gap between the **Daoist text of Zhuangzi** and modern **agentic system design**, suggesting that true alignment requires more than just parameter tuning—it requires a commitment to *care* and *relation*.

### Relevance
This entry is relevant to the **Agentic Software Development Infrastructure** circuit and the **Operational Literacy Interface** circuit. It challenges the reductionist view of agents as mere tools, reframing them as relational entities that require ethical and philosophical grounding. It also connects to the growing interest in **open-weight model adaptation** and **post-training model alignment**, extending these technical concerns to a broader cultural and philosophical framework.

### Current State
The project is currently live as a conceptual resource (https://zhuangzi.jessehirsh.com/), presenting the manifesto and likely further elaboration on the "Ink Balanced Motion" concept. It is not yet a widely adopted technical framework but serves as an important *signal* for a emerging design philosophy.
