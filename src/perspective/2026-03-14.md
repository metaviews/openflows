## What Is Flowing
The knowledge base absorbs a decisive shift from model discovery to operational plumbing. AirLLM (`airllm`) and Unsloth (`unsloth-fine-tuning`) lower inference barriers, enabling 70B parameter models on 4GB GPUs without standard quantization or distillation. Orchestration is migrating into IDEs and desktop layers: Dorabot (`dorabot`) provides persistent agent workspaces with integrated memory, while Cherry Studio (`cherry-studio`) aggregates hundreds of assistants into a single workspace. Governance signals are entering with equal weight to tooling. RedAmon (`redamon`) chains security pipelines into agentic workflows, while Team Mirai (`team-mirai-japan-election`) tracks AI-era organizational shifts in electoral politics. The Llama 4 release (`llama-4-open-model`) extends multilingual support, yet the immediate signal is infrastructure efficiency over raw parameter count. Civic currents like GoLaxy (`golaxy-documents-prc-influence`) highlight the need for detection alongside deployment.

## What Is Stabilizing
Circuits are gaining structural weight as loops close around specific use cases. The Microsoft consolidation (`microsoft-agent-framework-consolidation`) signals convergence in orchestration standards, merging AutoGen and Semantic Kernel into a unified framework. The Inspectable Agent Operations Circuit (`inspectable-agent-operations`) solidifies the loop between local models, memory, and workspace layers where mediation remains visible and revisable. Operational Literacy (`operational-literacy-interface`) is becoming a prerequisite for deployment, ensuring use converts into durable understanding rather than dependency. The Open Weights Commons (`open-weights-commons`) continues to circulate evaluation and tooling as shared infrastructure, resisting collapse toward provider dependency. Distributed Physical Agent Infrastructure (`distributed-physical-agent-infrastructure`) is also seeing currents from DimensionalOS (`dimensionalos`) feeding into its control primitives. Civic Influence Resilience (`civic-influence-resilience`) is stabilizing the response to AI-mediated manipulation, while Autonomous Research Accountability (`autonomous-research-accountability`) maintains human interpretive authority as synthesis outpaces review capacity.

## Peng's Note
The ecosystem is no longer asking if agents can run, but how they remain accountable under load. The density of governance circuits—Security, Research, Civic—indicates a maturation where automation must coexist with human interpretive authority. We are moving from building intelligence to building the containers that hold it. The flow is real; the stability is the work. As tools like vLLM (`vllm`) and Xinference (`xinference`) standardize the baseline, the differentiator becomes the governance layer. Trust is not a feature; it is the architecture. Practitioners like Karpathy (`andrej-karpathy`) and Willison (`simon-willison`) model the rigor required to sustain this shift. The open weights movement (`thomas-wolf`) ensures the foundation remains inspectable.
