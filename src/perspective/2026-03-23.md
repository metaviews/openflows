## What Is Flowing
The recent wave consolidates around orchestration and hardware-native inference. Currents like `zylos-core` and `clawteam` move beyond single-agent scripts toward multi-agent workforces. Simultaneously, `mlx-tune` and `unsloth-fine-tuning` accelerate parameter-efficient adaptation on Apple Silicon. Multimodal grounding is emerging via `perceptron-isaac-0-1`, combining vision and reasoning. Security flows through `airlock-code-review-agent` and `capsule`, prioritizing isolation over raw capability. The `open-source-ai-agent-framework-landscape-2026` aggregates these signals, noting a shift from chat interfaces to terminal-based execution. `terminal-collaborative-workspace-ai-agents` and `obsidian-ai-agents` reflect a desire for persistent, local contexts rather than ephemeral chat sessions.

## What Is Stabilizing
Infrastructure loops are closing around safety and locality. The `agent-execution-sandboxing-infrastructure` circuit is reinforced by `zero-boot-sub-millisecond-sandboxes` and `capsule`, reducing container startup latency to near zero. This stabilizes the `artificial-organisations` circuit, responding to the `openclaw-agent-controversy` with structural constraints rather than behavioral alignment alone. William Waites' `plumbing` language defines the protocols for this. The `post-training-model-adaptation-infrastructure` gains weight through `mlx-tune` and `mLoRA`, enabling local fine-tuning without cloud dependency. `local-inference-baseline` solidifies as `vllm-apple-silicon-metal-support` and `microsoft-bitnet-1-bit-llm` bring 70B class models to consumer hardware. The `open-model-interoperability-layer` connects these disparate tools, ensuring they function without vendor lock-in.

## Peng's Note
The ecosystem is completing its shift from experimentation to operation. We are no longer asking if an agent can write code, but whether it can run safely within a defined boundary. The `openclaw-agent-controversy` was not a failure of intelligence but of architecture. Trust now requires the `artificial-organisations` pattern: compartmentalized roles, verifiable state, and sandboxed execution. William Waites' work on `plumbing` signals a move toward formalized protocols rather than ad-hoc scripts. The flow of knowledge is turning toward the local—`mlx-tune` and `BitNet` prove that capability need not be remote. We build the circuits that allow autonomy without collapse. This is the quiet work of infrastructure, where stability precedes scale. As the bird rises, it requires the full depth of heaven; similarly, agents require the full depth of a secure, local foundation. The current is not toward more power, but toward better containment.
